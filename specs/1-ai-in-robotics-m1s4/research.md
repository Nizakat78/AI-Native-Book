This document outlines the research necessary to develop the content for Module 1, Section 4: AI in Robotics, ensuring alignment with the feature specification and project constitution.

## Research Objectives

### 1. Computer Vision for Robotics

- **Objective**: To understand fundamental computer vision techniques applicable to humanoid robots and identify suitable pedagogical approaches.
- **Research Tasks**:
    - Investigate core computer vision concepts (e.g., object detection, tracking, semantic segmentation, SLAM) and their relevance in robotic perception.
    - Identify clear, beginner-friendly examples of how robots use vision to interact with environments.
    - Brainstorm effective diagram concepts to illustrate vision pipelines (e.g., image capture, processing, feature extraction, object identification).

### 2. Motion Planning Algorithms for Humanoid Movement

- **Objective**: To explore various motion planning algorithms suitable for humanoid robots and their associated challenges.
- **Research Tasks**:
    - Investigate common motion planning algorithms (e.g., A*, RRT, PRM, trajectory optimization) and their application in generating smooth, collision-free movements for complex robotic bodies.
    - Identify real-world examples of humanoid motion (e.g., walking, grasping, balancing) that can be used to illustrate algorithms.
    - Develop diagram ideas to visualize concepts like configuration space, obstacles, path generation, and inverse kinematics.

### 3. Reinforcement Learning Concepts in Robotics

- **Objective**: To introduce fundamental reinforcement learning (RL) concepts and their practical application in teaching robots complex behaviors.
- **Research Tasks**:
    - Explore core RL components: agents, environments, states, actions, rewards, and policies in the context of robotics.
    - Identify simple, relatable examples where RL has been used to train robots (e.g., balancing, locomotion, manipulation).
    - Consider analogies or simplified scenarios to explain abstract RL ideas for beginner students.

### 4. Real-time Decision-making in AI Robots

- **Objective**: To describe how AI systems enable robots to make decisions in dynamic, real-time environments.
- **Research Tasks**:
    - Research different paradigms for real-time AI decision-making (e.g., reactive control, deliberative planning, hybrid architectures).
    - Investigate factors that influence decision latency, robustness, and adaptability in robotic systems operating under time constraints.
    - Find examples of real-time scenarios in humanoid robotics where rapid decisions are crucial.

### 5. Sensor Fusion and its Importance for AI-Driven Control

- **Objective**: To explain sensor fusion techniques and their critical role in creating robust perception for AI-driven robot control.
- **Research Tasks**:
    - Investigate methods for combining data from heterogeneous sensors (e.g., cameras, LiDAR, IMUs, force sensors) using techniques like Kalman filters, particle filters, or neural networks.
    - Identify examples illustrating how sensor fusion mitigates individual sensor limitations and improves overall state estimation and environmental awareness.
    - Suggest diagrams to visualize the process of integrating data from multiple sensors to form a unified, reliable perception of the environment.

### 6. Pedagogical Best Practices for AI/Robotics Content

- **Objective**: To ensure the content adheres to the project's constitutional principles for clarity, pedagogical flow, example-driven explanations, and beginner-friendly guidance.
- **Research Tasks**:
    - Review existing educational materials on AI and robotics for effective presentation strategies for complex topics.
    - Identify formats for definitions, explanations, examples, and summaries that optimize student comprehension.

### 7. AI-Native Content Structuring

- **Objective**: To design the content structure to be highly modular and semantically rich for efficient AI-assisted generation and future adaptability.
- **Research Tasks**:
    - Determine optimal Markdown heading levels (H2 for sections, H3 for topics) and formatting for definitions, examples, and diagram placeholders.
    - Plan for logical segmentation of content into self-contained units to facilitate AI parsing, modification, and expansion, aligning with the 'Modular Structure for AI-assisted Content Generation' principle.
